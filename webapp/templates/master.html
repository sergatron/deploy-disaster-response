In the below figure, the clusters represent messages which are very close related in terms of its subject. This can be easily seen when exploring the smaller clusters around the perimeter of the plot.

As you can also see, there is significant overlap between some clusters. This overlap occurs because most messages have more than one category as there are 34 categories in total. For example, a single message can be classified as having a need for food, water, and shelter. In this case, there are three subjects being mentioned and thus creates a greater chance for overlap between three clusters.
